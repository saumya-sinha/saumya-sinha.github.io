I am a postdoc at Rice University, working with Prof. Andrew Schaefer in the areas of mixed-integer programming and organ transplantation. Prior to this, I received a PhD in applied mathematics under the supervision of Prof. Archis Ghate at the University of Washington, Seattle. My research focuses on problems of decision-making under uncertainty, motivated primarily by applications in healthcare operations, health policy and inventory control. Here's a summary of my research interests:

  • Incentive design in healthcare

    I use tools like simulation, optimization and game theory to study the effectiveness of policies of US healthcare agencies (e.g., CMS) in incentivizing societally desriable behavior on the part of individual actors, such as hospitals and physicians. I am a visitng postdoctoral fellow at Houston Methodist Hospital (HMH), and I work closely with surgeons at HMH to assess the strategic response of transplant programs to outcome-based regulations in organ transplantation.

  • Robust dynamic programming

    I develop theory and solution methods for robust dynamic programs, and study their application in inventory control and treatment planning. During my PhD, I developed approximate solution methods for certain classes of infinite-dimentional robust Markov decision-processes that cannot be solved using standard methods. On the applied side, I developed a robust Newsvendor model for inventory management of a product over multiple periods in the face of ambiguous demand distributions. I also proposed a robust extension of response-guided treatment planning for rheumatoid arthritis.
